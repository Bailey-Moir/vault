If all possible outcomes are equally likely, the [[Probabilities|probability]] of an [[Events|event]] $A$ is
$$ P(A) = \dfrac{\text{\# of outcomes in $A$}}{\text{Total \# of outcomes}} $$

> [!example]-
> If we toss a fair coin twice, the [[Probability|probability]] that both tosses give the same outcome is
> $$P(HH,TT) = \dfrac{2}{4} = \dfrac{1}{2}$$
> Here $A = \{HH, TT\}$ and $\ohm = \{HH, HT, TH, TT\}$
