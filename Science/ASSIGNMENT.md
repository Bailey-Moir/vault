**Writing**
There is debate about whether or not Mathematics is a science. The main point brought up against Mathematics being a science is the lack of experimentation and the lack of link to the real world. Mathematics has the unique ability to branch off from scientific studies (applied mathematics) into mathematics for its own sake (abstract mathematics). The largest point of difference between Mathematics as a science and other disciplines that I personally see is how ideas are formed. In most other disciplines, scientists see a problem in the real world that needs to be solved and chose what to work on based on this, or at least when they do work, their work is linked to real world phenomenon. Contrastly, mathematicians often find gaps or unknowns in mathematics and attempt to fill them in, with no obvious link between their work and the real world, both in conception up to publishing.
