The *Lambert $W$ function* $W(x)$ is defined as the [[Math/Discrete/Set Theory/Functions/Inverse Functions|inverse function]] of $f(x)=xe^x$.
- $W(xe^x) = x$
- $W(x)e^{W(x)} = x$

> [!note]- [[Math/Discrete/Set Theory/Functions/Domain|Domains]] and [[Math/Discrete/Set Theory/Functions/Range|Ranges]]
>  ![500|center](lambert-w-inverse-graph.excalidraw)
>  In order for $f$ to have an [[Math/Discrete/Set Theory/Functions/Inverse Functions|inverse]], it is restricted to the [[Math/Discrete/Set Theory/Functions/Domain|domain]] of $\left[ -1,\infty \right)$ as to be [[Injective Functions|injective]], with a [[Math/Discrete/Set Theory/Functions/Range|range]] of $\left[-\frac{1}{e},\infty\right)$. Thus, $W: \left[-\frac{1}{e},\infty\right) \to \left[ -1,\infty \right)$.
