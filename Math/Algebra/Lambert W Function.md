The _Lambert $W$ function_ $W(x)$ is defined as the [[Inverse Functions|inverse function]] of $f(x)=xe^x$.

$$W(xe^x) = x$$

- $W(xe^x) = x$
- $W(x)e^{W(x)} = x$

> [!note]- [[Domain|Domains]] and [[Range & Image|Ranges]]
> ![500|center](lambert-w-inverse-graph.excalidraw.md)
> In order for $f$ to have an [[Inverse Functions|inverse]], it is restricted to the [[Domain|domain]] of $\left[ -1,\infty \right)$ as to be [[Injective Functions|injective]], with a [[Range & Image|range]] of $\left[-\frac{1}{e},\infty\right)$. Thus, $W: \left[-\frac{1}{e},\infty\right) \to \left[ -1,\infty \right)$.
