Sometimes called the scalar or inner product, the dot product is found by multiplying corresponding components. Dot products have many purposes, but their most basic is to [[Orthogonal Vectors|orthogonality]].
$$\begin{align*}
	\mathbf{\underline{a}} \cdot \mathbf{\underline{b}} &= a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + \cdots \\
	&= \left| \mathbf{\underline{a}} \right| \left| \mathbf{\underline{b}} \right| \cos \theta && \theta \text{ is the angle between } \mathbf{\underline{a}} \text{ and } \mathbf{\underline{b}}
\end{align*}$$
