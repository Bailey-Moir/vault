To prove an [[Argument|argument]]/theorem of the form $p \to q$^[[[Implication]]] we assume $p$ is true and derive $q$.

To prove an [[Argument|argument]]/theorem of the form $p \leftrightarrow q$^[[[Biconditional]]] we attempt to prove $p \to q$ [[Conjunction|and]] $q \to p$.
