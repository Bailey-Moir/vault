- A vertex/node (henceforth called a vertex) $w$ is said to be **adjacent** to vertex $v$ if the graph contains an [[Edges|edge]] $(v,w)$.
- The **neighbourhood** of a vertex $v$ is a subgraph formed by all vertices adjacent to $v$.
- The **degree** of a vertex $v$, denoted $\delta(v)$, is the number of [[Edges|edges]] in its neighbourhood. An **isolated vertex** is a vertex with degree zero.
- The **vertex set** of a graph $G$ is denoted by $V(G)$.
