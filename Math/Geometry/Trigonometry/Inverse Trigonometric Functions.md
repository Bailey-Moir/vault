None of the trigonometric functions are [[Injective Functions|one-to-one]], meaning that in order to have an [[Inverse Functions|inverse]], their domain must be restricted.

|                       | $\large\sin^{-1}$                               | $\large\cos^{-1}$       | $\large\tan^{-1}$                               |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **[[Range & Image]]** | $\left[ -\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{\pi}{2} \right]$ | $\left[ 0, \pi \right]$ | $\left( -\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{\pi}{2} \right)$ |

> [!example]- Inverse Sine
> For example, $\sin$ is [[Injective Functions|one-to-one]] on the [[Intervals|interval]] $\left[ -\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{\pi}{2} \right]$, so $\sin^{-1}$ is defined with this restriction.
> $$ x = \sin^{-1} y \iff y = \sin x \text{ s.t. } x \in \left[ -\dfrac{\pi}{2}, \dfrac{\pi}{2} \right] $$
