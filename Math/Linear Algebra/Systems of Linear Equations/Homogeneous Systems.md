A system where all of the constants on the RHS are 0; $A\mathbf{\underline{x}} = \mathbf{\underline{0}}$
- In these systems, there will always be **at least one solution: the trivial solution** at $\mathbf{\underline{x}} = \mathbf{\underline{0}}$.
- Homogeneous systems are **always consistent**.
- If there are no free variables, the solution set must just be $\mathbf{\underline{0}}$, as $|S| \in \{0, 1, \infty\}$.
