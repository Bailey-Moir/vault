Sometimes called the *multiplicative identity*, identity matrices are square matrices denoted as $I$ or $I_n$ where the only non-zero entries are the ones in the main diagonal.
Multiplying any given matrix by it's identity matrix produces the same matrix, making it the [[Matrix Multiplication|matrix multiplication]] equivalent of multiplying by 1.
- $AI_n \equiv I_mA \equiv A$
   where $A = m \times n$
$$ I_n = n \times n$$
$$\begin{array}{c c c c}
	I_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \end{bmatrix} &
	I_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} &
	I_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} &
	\dots 
\end{array}$$
