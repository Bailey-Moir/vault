$$
\begin{bmatrix}
	1 & 2 & 3 \\
	2 & 4 & 6 \\ 
	3 & 6 & 1	
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Symmetric matrices are the same across the main diagonal, having the unique property that $A = A^T$, i.e.

> [!proof]- $AA^T$ and $A^TA$ are symmetric
> $$\begin{align*}
> 	(AA^T)^T &= (A^T)^T(A)^T \\
>	 &= AA^T \\
>	 \\
> 	(A^TA)^T &= (A)^T(A^T)^T \\
> 	&= A^TA \\
> \end{align*}$$
