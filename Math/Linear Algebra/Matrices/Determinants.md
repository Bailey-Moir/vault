The determinant is a measure of how much a matrix scales when multiplying another matrix, thus the determinant of a scalar is itself (see [[Matrix Scalar Multiplication|multiplication of matrices by scalars]]).
$$\left| A \right| = \text{det}\:A$$

If any rows are redundant (a linear combination of the other rows) then the determinant is always zero.
