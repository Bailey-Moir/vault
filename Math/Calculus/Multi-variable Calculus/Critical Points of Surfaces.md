If $z=f(x,y)$ has a relative extremum at $(x_0,y_0)$ and the first order [[Partial Derivatives|partial derivatives]] exist at this point,
$$\begin{array}{ccc}
	f_x(x_0,y_0) = 0 &
	\text{and} &
	f_y(x_0,y_0) = 0
\end{array}$$
A point on a surface is a [[Critical Points|critical point]] iff $f_x=0$ and $f_y=0$, or at least one of the partial derivatives doesn't exist.
