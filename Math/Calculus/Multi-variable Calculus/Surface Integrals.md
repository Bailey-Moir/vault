 A parametric curve $\mathbf{r}(t)$ has a tangent vector $\mathbf{r}'(t)$. The parametric representation of a sruface is:
 $$ \mathbf{r}(u,v) = \langle x(u,v), y(u,v), z(u,v) \rangle $$
 Two tangent vector fields are $\mathbf{r}_u = \dfrac{\partial \mathbf{r}}{\partial u}$ and $\mathbf{r}_v = \dfrac{\partial \mathbf{r}}{\partial v}$; a normal vector field of $\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v$.
 $$ \iint_\sigma dS = \iint_R \left\| \mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v \right\| \, dA $$
