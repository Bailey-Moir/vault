In vector calculus, the gradient of a [[Scalar Fields|scalar field]] can be defined using the [[Nabla Operator|nabla operator]]:
$$ \text{grad}\,f = \nabla f = \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}\mathbf{\underline{\hat{e}}_1} + \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\mathbf{\underline{\hat{e}}_2} + \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial z}\mathbf{\underline{\hat{e}}_3}$$
- The gradient always points in direction of greatest increase of $f$.
- The gradient has a magnitude equal to the maximum rate of change at the point.
