Let $a,b,c$ be in a [[Groups#^abelian-groups|commutative]] [[Rings|ring]] such that $ab = ac$. Then if $a\ne 0$ is not a [[Zero-divisors|zero-divisor]], then $b = c$.

> [!proof]-
> $$\begin{align}
> 	ab &= ac \\
> 	ab - ac &= 0 \\
> 	a(b - c) &= 0 \\
> 	\to b - c &= 0 \\
> \end{align}$$
