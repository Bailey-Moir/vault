For $(G, *)$ to be a *group*, it must have the following properties
**Closure**
$$\begin{array}{l}
	\forall a,b \in G, \exists! c \in G \ni \\
	a*b=c
\end{array}$$
**Associativity**
$$\begin{array}{l}
	\forall a,b,c \in G,\\
	(a*b)*c = a*(b*c)
\end{array}$$
**Identity**
$$\begin{array}{l}
	\exists! e \in G \ni \forall a \in G, \\
	a*e = a = e*a
\end{array}$$
**Inverse**
$$\begin{array}{l}
	\forall a \in G, \exists! a^{-1} \in G \ni \\
	a * a^{-1} = e = a^{-1} * a
\end{array}$$

> [!note] Abelian Groups
> $\forall a,b \in G, (a*b = b*a)$ ($(G,*)$ has commutativity)
